From WIC to Lit

Childhood Rights Activist | Building Frameworks for Digital-Environmental Justice

I'm a mixed Black Indigenous Chicanx daughter of migrants from Louisiana and immigrants from Mexico, born and raised in South Central Los Angeles. As an Aborisa, Hoodoo Culture Worker, and mixed media artist, I design protective infrastructure for children navigating digital and environmental systems. My work spans trust & safety, OSINT investigations, and policy strategy—while my art interrogates the liminal spaces of Black-Indigenous-Mexican existence in the Americas.

What I Do

Design community-centered safety frameworks, map digital risk, and build participatory learning tools that keep children, caregivers, and educators informed.

How I Work

Blend OSINT, service design, and policy storytelling to translate complex threats into actionable playbooks for schools, nonprofits, and civic partners.

Who I Support

Black, Latinx, Indigenous, and multilingual communities navigating algorithmic harm, climate risk, and systemic inequity, starting with my own family.
