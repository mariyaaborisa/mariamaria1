From WIC to Lit

Children's Rights Advocate | Building Global Frameworks for Digital Safety & Environmental Resilience

In the words of the great poet Belcalis—Cardi B—I went from WIC to lit. I'm a mixed Black Indigenous Chicanx daughter of migrants from Louisiana and immigrants from Mexico, raised in South Central Los Angeles, and that trajectory shapes how I move through work and community.

From WIC to Lit is the framework that grounds my practice in childhood rights and digital-environmental justice. As an Aborisa, Hoodoo culture worker, and mixed media artist, I design protective infrastructure for children navigating digital and environmental systems, drawing from the same resourcefulness that carried my family forward.

My work spans trust & safety, OSINT investigations, and policy strategy, while my art interrogates the liminal spaces of Black-Indigenous-Mexican existence in the Americas. Every engagement centers community care, actionable playbooks, and culturally grounded storytelling.

What I Do

Design community-centered safety frameworks, map digital risk, and build participatory learning tools that keep children, caregivers, and educators informed.

How I Work

Blend OSINT, service design, and policy storytelling to translate complex threats into actionable playbooks for schools, nonprofits, and civic partners.

Who This Work Serves

Children and families in historically marginalized communities—Black, Latinx, Indigenous, immigrant, and multilingual populations—who face compounded risks from technology exploitation, climate threats, and structural barriers to safety. This work starts with my own family and extends to any community denied the fundamental right to protection.
